Yesterday at 11:34 am

I've been trying to find words for this for two days. Crossing that finish line — hearing them say your name over the PA in the dark, with the whole town cheering — is the kind of thing that doesn't fit inside a sentence. We all trained for this separately and somehow arrived at the same finish chute within eight minutes of each other. Eight minutes. After 140 miles.

AL
Andreas Louca
Yesterday at 9:08 am

Finally writing this down. I've been staring at the finisher's photo on my phone every morning since we got back. There's a moment in every Ironman — usually somewhere around mile 16 of the run — where you have a long, honest conversation with yourself about who you actually are. The lava fields don't let you look anywhere else. I thought about every early morning I'd spent on a trainer, every long ride in the rain, every time I told myself this was a reasonable way to spend a weekend.

And then I thought about the four of us standing on that beach at 6:45am in the dark, not saying much. That's the part I'll remember longest.
