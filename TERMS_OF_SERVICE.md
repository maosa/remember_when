## 1. What Remember When is

Remember When is a private, shared memory book. Users create "moments" — a name, an optional date, location, and tags — and invite specific other people to contribute posts (text, photos, videos, and voice recordings) to that moment. Moments are never public. There is no public feed, no likes, no follower counts, and no algorithmic recommendation of your content to strangers.

## 3. Moments, roles, and permissions

Content in Remember When lives inside "moments," and every person on a moment has one of three roles. These roles determine what a person can do — **not** who owns the underlying content (see Section 4).

- **Owner** — the person who created the moment (or who ownership has since been transferred to). Owners have full control: they can edit the moment's details, manage every member's role, remove members, invite new editors or readers, transfer ownership to an editor, and — if they choose to leave — either hand off ownership first or delete the moment for everyone.
- **Editor** — can post to the moment, edit the moment's details and tags, invite and remove readers, and delete any post in the moment (their own or someone else's). Editors cannot remove other editors or the owner, and cannot transfer ownership.
- **Reader** — view-only access. Readers can see the moment's posts and media but cannot post, edit, or manage membership. Readers are invited by the owner or any editor, or can join via a time-limited shareable link.

Roles can change over time (e.g., a reader promoted to editor), and role changes, removals, and ownership transfers trigger a notification to the people affected.

## 5. Inviting others

- If you invite someone by email and they don't have an account, we'll email them an invitation to sign up. Their email address is used only to facilitate that invitation and is not shared with other users.

Anyone who joins via a valid link before it's revoked keeps their access even after the link itself is invalidated.
